About
  At Rochester Institute of Technology, we shape the future and improve the world through creativity and innovation. We are an engaged, intellectually curious, and socially conscious community that’s leveraging the power of technology, the arts, and design for the greater good.
  Rochester Institute of Technology is located in Henrietta, Rochester, NY. It was founded in 1829, and is one of two technology institutes in New York. RIT's mascot is Ritchie the Tiger (RIT-chie).

Courses
RIT offers many different subjects across many different fields of study. However, RIT is, as an institute of technology, primarily focused on digital, engineering, and "hardware" skills, but unusually has an artistic twist in the form of very good film, animation, and game design programs.

One of RIT's well-known successes is the implementation of co-ops, which are paid internships that frequently land jobs after college, and are integral to many of RIT's courses.

Campus
RIT is located in Henrietta, Rochester, New York. It has a suburban campus with plenty of green and light. Due to the coldness of the winter, a tunnel network exists below the campus to allow for subterranean transit when the outside ground is icy.

RIT is also right next to Lake Erie, and due to this is receives a high snowfall but also has classes in Kayaking and Flyfishing.

RIT has multiple housing options for students participating in different programs and in-school colleges, with some being "suite style" and others "apartment style". The typical cost of housing at RIT is roughly $5,000 per semester with slight fluctuations for style of room and number of occupants.

There are several eating places on Campus besides the cafeteria, and there is also a tech-repair shop. The SHED is an enormous makerspace available to all majors.
